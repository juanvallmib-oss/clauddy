What makes it good

Not a spec sheet — just the stuff that actually matters day to day.

📷

Camera

Honestly the main reason most people upgrade. The computational photography is witchcraft at this point.

🔋

Battery life

The 15 Pro Max finally lasts a full day. Took them long enough, but it's genuinely good now.

🔒

Privacy

Not perfect, but Apple's stance on privacy is miles ahead of the competition. Face ID is fast and secure.

🧩

Ecosystem

AirDrop, Handoff, iMessage — if you're already on a Mac, it just clicks. Lock-in, sure, but it works.

♻️

Longevity

5–6 years of iOS updates. My dad is still on an iPhone 11 and it runs great. Hard to beat.

⚡

Chip

The A17 Pro benchmarks laptop CPUs. Whether you'll ever need that power is another question.

A quick history

60 words or less per entry, I promise.

2007 — iPhone (1st gen)

No copy-paste. No App Store. No 3G. And yet it felt like the future. Touchscreen changed everything overnight.

2010 — iPhone 4

Retina display, glass back, steel band. Still one of the best-looking phones ever made. Also antennagate happened. Moving on.

2017 — iPhone X

Bye bye home button, hello Face ID and the notch. The design language we still basically live with today.

2020 — iPhone 12

5G arrives. Flat edges return. MagSafe makes a comeback. Arguably the best design refresh in a decade.

2023 — iPhone 15 Pro

Titanium body, Action Button, USB-C finally (RIP Lightning), and a periscope zoom on the Max. Solid all-rounder.

Look, I'm not going to pretend iPhones are perfect. They're expensive. The charger situation was annoying for years. And yes, the notch was ugly.

But there's a reason these things hold their value, get years of software support, and make people genuinely reluctant to switch. The hardware and software integration is just tighter than anything else out there right now.

If you're already in the Apple world — MacBook, AirPods, Apple Watch — an iPhone is a no-brainer. If you're not, it's a harder sell. Android has come a long way. But the iPhone still sets the bar everyone else chases.

— A person who has owned way too many phones
